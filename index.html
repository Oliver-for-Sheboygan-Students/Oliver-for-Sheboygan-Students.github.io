Goals

Roger is committed to focusing our schools on academics. We must focus education on reading, writing and math skills so our students can become successful in their personal and professional lives.

Students

Education must focus on developing the verbal and numeric skills to analyze and to make conclusions based on logic and evidence. The decision to include any values instructions that competes with the development of these skills must be supported by hard data that it is actually needed. It must also be regularly evaluated to determine if it is producing the desired and published results.

Teachers

Reward good teachers: Student performance should be evaluated objectively. Where we are failing, we should seek creative means to improve and review our policies to eliminate those that may be holding our students back or keeping talented and dedicated teachers from adapting to a child's needs. Dropping our standards to look good shortchanges the students, parents, teachers and the future of our community.

Encourage teacher creativity: Teachers should feel freedom to work with parents and experiment with methods that work best for individual children.

Taxpayers

Parents and taxpayers expect transparency and accountability in the use of funds and that they will not be paying for programs that are contrary to their values and morals.

Before burdening taxpayers with more taxes, let's focus on improving our schools' output.

Funding must reach the classroom and reward good teachers.

Parents

Listen to parents and teachers to champion their children's education.

Responsible parents understand that they are the sole determiners of their children’s upbringing.

Parents are in charge of raising their child how they want them to be raised. This is normal. It’s more than normal—it’s law. In Washington v. Glucksberg, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled that the Fourteenth Amendment protects the fundamental authority of parents to direct the care, upbringing, and education of their children.

Parental authority

Responsible parents understand that they are the sole determiners of their children’s upbringing.

Parents are in charge of raising their child how they want them to be raised. This is normal. It’s more than normal—it’s law. In Washington v. Glucksberg, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled that the Fourteenth Amendment protects the fundamental authority of parents to direct the care, upbringing, and education of their children.

The most ancient, intimate and tender human relationship is that of a child to his or her parents. The educational establishment does not have the right to break or undermine this relationship.

Parents are the primary authority in a child's life and the educational establishment should not presume to usurp that role or undermine parental authority.

Parents have the authority to choose how and where their students will be educated.

Parents have the authority to demand to be informed about what their children are being taught. Information about what is being taught must be transparent and easily accessible.

The process for a parent to opt out of values or ideological instruction for their children must be easily accessible and simple. No hoops to jump through. Parental decisions must be respected.

Parental responsibility

Parents are responsible to help their children with their homework and be aware of their progress.

Parents are responsible to communicate with their student's teachers and work with the teachers.

Parents are responsible for the discipline of their children to support the school's rules and procedures.
